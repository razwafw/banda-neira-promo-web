Getting to Banda Neira

5 minutes read Written on February 18th, 2024

Banda Neira is a remote island in the Banda Islands of Indonesia, known for its historical significance in the spice trade and its natural beauty. However, getting to Banda Neira can be a bit challenging due to its remote location and limited transportation options. Below are the steps that you can expect to take to get to Banda Neira.

Step 1: Travel to Ambon

Getting to Ambon first is a common way to get to Banda Island. To get to Ambon, Indonesia, you typically have a few options depending on your starting location and travel preferences.


Photo of Pattimura International Airport

The easiest way to get to Ambon is by air through Pattimura International Airport. This airport serves several domestic connections to other major aiports in Indonesia, including Jakarta, Bali, and Medan. It also accomodates international flights from Malaysia, Singapore, and other countries. One way flight fares range from Rp1.750.000,00 to more than Rp3.000.000,00 (depending on departure location). Step 2: Get to Banda Islands

After arriving to Ambon, the next step is to goal is to arrive at Banda Islands. To do this, there are a few options:

Step 2a: Using Susi Air or Sam Air

Travelers have the option to utilize Susi Air for a convenient 40-minute flight from Ambon directly to Banda Neira. While the airline has faced criticism and is prohibited from operating within the European Union due to safety concerns, it remains a preferred choice for many local hotel owners traveling to business meetings in Jakarta or Surabaya. Feedback from these individuals reflects positive experiences specifically on the Ambon to Banda Neira route, reassuring passengers about the reliability and safety of this service. Additionally, the adequate length of the airstrip on Banda Neira alleviates any apprehension regarding landing and takeoff. For those seeking efficiency in travel, Susi Air presents a viable alternative to the slower local ferry, albeit contingent on operational reliability. Another alternative is Sam Air, which currently also serves the route.


Example of a Susi Air flight schedule for Maluku Province

To procure tickets for the flight, travelers have two options: either purchasing them directly at Ambon airport or arranging through a local contact. It is important to note that the one way fare for a flight to Bandanaira Airport usually costs around Rp350.000,00. It's imperative to stay updated on the latest flight schedules as they undergo revisions every six months, with a minimum frequency of two flights per week to and from Banda Neira. Streamlining the process, securing accommodation on Banda Neira first and subsequently coordinating with the lodging to reserve plane tickets is advised. However, it's crucial to acknowledge the unpredictability inherent in air travel to these remote destinations. Flights may be subject to cancellation due to constraints such as limited aircraft availability, pilots, and passenger capacity. Therefore, flexibility and a contingency travel plan are essential prerequisites for those intending to fly. Step 2b: Using the Pelni Ferry

Front gates of Banda Naira Port

The more reliable, although less convenient, approach is to take the Pelni Ferry. The government-run company Pelni is always reliable and helps the locals to travel everywhere in Indonesia for a very reasonable price. The ticket is only around Rp140.000,00 per person from Ambon to Banda, just go to the ticket office and buy your ticket after you arrive in Ambon or book it from their website. The Pelni is never cancelled so you can rely on the information online. The ride will take between 12 to 19 hours depending on the direction and the waves.
